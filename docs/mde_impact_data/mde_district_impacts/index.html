    Mississippi Department of Education (MDE) District Impact Data Overview #

    What is “Impact Data”? #

    This Mississippi Department of Education (MDE) releases impact data each year. After MAAP (Mississippi Academic Assessment Program) testing in April and May, the MDE begins releasing data in June and ends with the final impact data around August or September.

    700 Point Impact Data Overview #

    Here is the 700 point school (elementary and middle schools) impact data template:

    700 pt Reading Math Science
    Proficiency 100 pts 100 pts 100 pts
    Growth of All 100 pts 100 pts Paticipation Rate
    Growth of LPS 100 pts 100 pts >=95%

    700 point schools are held accountable for thier student’s performance in English/ language arts (ELA), mathematics, and science (5th and/or 8th grade). The majority of 700 points schools serve at least grades 3 through 8.

    For the proficiency row, you can score up to 100 points for each subject (ELA, math, and science).

    For the growth of all row, you can score up to 125 points for math and ELA only. The reason you can score up to 125 points is because you could see where all of your students grew at least 2 levels or grew to the advanced proficiency level.

    For the growth of LPS (low performing students), you can score up to 125 points for math and ELA only. The reasong you can score up to 125 points is the same as the growth of all students category. The different is the the LPS students are predetermined by the MDE. Students that fall into the LPS category are the “bottom 25%” for their grade level and subject.

    For more information, please visit the “Accountability Model” section of the support site. This will provide you with an indepth look at the MDE accountability model with examples and scenarios.

    1000 Point Impact Data Overview #

    Here is the 1000 point school (high schools and districts) impact data template:

    1000 pt Reading Math Science History Acceleration
    Proficiency 100 pts 100 pts 50 pts 50 pts 50 pts
    Growth of All 100 pts 100 pts Graduation Rate Participation Rate CCR Readiness Rate
    Growth of LPS 100 pts 100 pts up to 200 points >=95% up to 50 pts

    1000 point schools are held accountable for their student’s performance in English/ language arts (ELA), mathematics, science (8th grade and/or Biology I), history (US History). The majority of 1000 point schools serve grades 9 - 12. There are some high schools that are 1000 points and serve grades 7 - 12 as well as some attendance centers that serve K-12. If your school falls into any of these categories, please click here.

    Using the impact data from MAAP testing, the MDE assigns your school and district an “A” through “F” letter grade. All users for each school is able to see thier schools impact data because this data is open to the public.

    Equity Analytics allows you to view all impact data for your school from school year 2015-2016 to present. The MDE started recording impact data in 2016, when the MS transistioned to the new state standards and cooresponding accountbility model.
